Halloween History

Gaelic and Welsh Influence

  • Pagan roots
  • Samhain/Calan Gaeaf marked the end of the harvest season and beginning of winter or the 'darker half' of the year.
  • People going house-to-house in costume (or in disguise), usually reciting verses or songs in exchange for food in the festival included mumming and guising in Ireland, Scotland, the Isle of Man and Wales.

Christian Influence

  • The full name of All Hallows' Eve
  • Halloween is the evening before the Christian holy days of All Hallows' Day on 1 November and All Souls' Day on 2 November
  • Many Christians in mainland Europe, especially in France, believed "that once a year, on Hallowe'en, the dead of the churchyards rose for one wild, hideous carnival" known as the danse macabre, which has often been depicted in church decoration.
